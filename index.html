About the Lab

My research centres on the development and application of intelligent technological solutions—specifically AI, wearable sensors, and robotics—to address critical challenges in healthcare, with a primary focus on neurological disorders and elderly care. The core of my work involves using a data-driven approach to objectively assess, monitor, and improve health outcomes.

Core Research Areas

My expertise is concentrated in the following interdisciplinary domains:

  • I specialize in utilizing wearable sensors and IMU sensors for continuous, non-invasive monitoring of physiological and kinetic parameters.
  • Objective assessment of biomechanical parameters for the classification and grading of neurological conditions.
  • Monitoring sleep, heart rate, and spontaneous activity in various populations, including older adults and caregivers of people with dementia, using multi-sensor wearable systems.
  • Developing and applying data-driven models and machine learning/deep learning algorithms to clinical data.
  • Using techniques like Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (Bi-LSTM) for disease classification, and classification algorithms for intrusion & activity detection.
  • Applying AI to analyze complex signals, such as ambient acoustic events for smart homes and speech assessment for conditions like hypokinetic dysarthria.
  • PhD and post-doctoral work at The BioRobotics Institute, focusing on practical application of technology to therapy.
  • Experience developing systems for kinesiotherapy and rehabilitation; assessing movements for post-stroke patients in Activities of Daily Living (ADL).
  • Using devices like the Leap Motion controller for objective assessment of upper limb motor skills in Parkinson's disease.
  • Using AI for environmental challenges, such as deforestation prediction using Geo-Spatial data and analysing tree cover loss impacts on carbon emissions.
  • Projects on computer vision tasks like semantic segmentation and object detection (YOLO) over aerial images.
